## Brand Overview

### Mission Statement

Deets exists to eliminate the friction between meeting someone and staying connected. We believe networking should be effortless, private, and human.

### Brand Positioning

**Tagline**: "Meet once. Remember always."

**Positioning Statement**:
For professionals who value genuine connections, Deets is the business card scanner that respects your privacy while keeping relationships alive. Unlike CRM tools that feel corporate or cloud scanners that compromise your data, Deets keeps everything on your device and gets out of your way.

### Brand Personality

**Confident**: We know privacy matters. We don't apologize for doing things differently.
**Human**: We talk like real people, not a corporate press release.
**Clear**: No jargon, no confusion. Scan, save, done.
**Trustworthy**: Privacy-first isn't a feature. It's who we are.
**Efficient**: We respect your time. Every screen has a purpose.

### Brand Values

1. **Privacy Above All**: Your contacts belong to you. Period.
2. **Simplicity in Execution**: Complex tech, simple experience.
3. **Human Connection**: Technology should strengthen relationships, not replace them.
4. **Transparency**: No hidden agendas. No data harvesting. No BS.
5. **Quality Over Speed**: Ship when it's right, not when it's rushed.

## Logo System

### Primary Logo: The Interlocking Dots

The Deets logo consists of two overlapping circles forming an abstract "D" shape. This represents:
- **Connection**: Two entities (people) meeting
- **Privacy**: Distinct, separate entities maintaining boundaries
- **Continuity**: The overlap creates something new (a relationship)


### Logo Variations

#### 1. Full Color (Primary)
- Use on white or light backgrounds
- Teal dots (#23C4AE) at 100% opacity
- Minimum size: 32px Ã— 32px (digital), 0.5" Ã— 0.5" (print)

#### 2. Monochrome Dark
- Use on light backgrounds when color isn't available
- Graphite (#2B2E3A) at 100% opacity
- Maintains same geometric proportions

#### 3. Monochrome Light (White)
- Use on dark backgrounds or photos
- Pure white (#FFFFFF) at 100% opacity
- Always include subtle glow/shadow for visibility

#### 4. App Icon (iOS)
- Teal gradient background (#23C4AE to #1FA896)
- White dots at 90% opacity
- 1024Ã—1024px master file
- Follows iOS Human Interface Guidelines
- No text, icon only

### Logo Spacing

**Clear Space**: Minimum clear space around logo = height of one dot
**Alignment**: Logo should always be optically centered
**Pairing with Text**: When next to "Deets" wordmark, use 0.5Ã— clear space between

### Incorrect Logo Usage (Don't)

- Don't rotate the logo
- Don't change dot colors independently
- Don't add effects (drop shadows, glows, bevels)
- Don't place on busy backgrounds without a frame
- Don't stretch or distort proportions
- Don't use low-resolution versions

### Color Application Guidelines

**Hierarchy**:
1. Teal for primary actions (what you want users to do)
2. Coral for secondary highlights (favorites, notifications)
3. Graphite for content and structure
4. Mist for backgrounds and breathing room

**Contrast Ratios** (WCAG AAA):
- Body text on background: Minimum 7:1
- UI elements on background: Minimum 4.5:1
- Icons and borders: Minimum 3:1

**Dark Mode Adaptations**:
- Teal: Lighten to `#2DD4BD` (+10% brightness)
- Coral: Lighten to `#FF8B82` (+10% brightness)
- Graphite: Use `#E5E7EB` for text on dark backgrounds
- Mist: Becomes `#1F2937` (dark surface)

**Dynamic Type Support**: Always support iOS Dynamic Type (accessibility)
**Line Length**: 50-75 characters for readability

**Hierarchy**:
- Use weight, not size alone, to create hierarchy
- Never use more than 3 font sizes on one screen
- Maintain consistent spacing ratios (multiples of 8pt)

### Tone Variations by Context

#### Onboarding (Welcoming)
- **Goal**: Make new users feel capable
- **Tone**: Warm, encouraging, brief
- **Example**: "Welcome to Deets. Scan your first card to get started."

#### Empty States (Encouraging)
- **Goal**: Prompt action without feeling pushy
- **Tone**: Friendly, light, actionable
- **Example**: "No cards yet. Tap the scan button to add your first contact."

#### Errors (Apologetic but Clear)
- **Goal**: Explain what went wrong and how to fix it
- **Tone**: Honest, helpful, not dramatic
- **Example**: "Couldn't save contact. Check your storage and try again."

#### Settings (Informative)
- **Goal**: Help users make informed choices
- **Tone**: Transparent, educational, neutral
- **Example**: "iCloud Sync keeps contacts in sync across your devices. All data is encrypted by Apple."

#### Success States (Affirming)
- **Goal**: Confirm action without over-celebrating
- **Tone**: Simple, positive, brief
- **Example**: "Contact saved."

### Writing Guidelines

**Sentence Structure**:
- Start with verbs (imperative mood): "Scan a card", "Export to Contacts"
- Keep sentences under 20 words
- One idea per sentence
- Use active voice ("Deets scans cards" not "Cards are scanned by Deets")

**Word Choice**:
- Use "you" and "your" (personal)
- Avoid jargon ("OCR" â†’ "scan", "export" is okay)
- Be specific ("3 contacts" not "some contacts")
- No filler words ("just", "simply", "very")

**Punctuation**:
- Use periods for complete sentences
- Skip periods for buttons and short labels
- Use contractions (we're, you're, it's) to sound human
- Avoid exclamation marks unless genuinely exciting

## Appendix: Design Principles

### Simplicity
Every screen should have one primary action. If you can't identify it in 3 seconds, the design is too complex.

### Privacy by Design
Never ask for permissions until the moment they're needed. Always explain why before requesting.

### Accessibility First
Every UI element must work with VoiceOver. Every color must pass contrast checks. Dynamic Type is non-negotiable.

### Respect Time
Users are busy. Get them in, get them done, get them out. No unnecessary steps.

### Human Touch
Design like you're talking to a friend. Clear labels, helpful hints, forgiving errors.
